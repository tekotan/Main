Mechanical Winch

Duration: October 14, 2019 - October 23, 2019
Partners: Oleg Bychenkov, Kaushal Ramalingam, and Kabir Shah
Objective: Create a mechanical winch to lift at least 100 g for 30 cm
Details: For this project, we as a team had to create a mechanical winch from Vex parts which would be able to lift 100 g for 30 cm. To power our winch, we had access to a FisherTech motor and 7.5 V of DC power. Since my teammates and I were all inexperienced in building machines, I took charge of the team to create the actual winch, and gave two other people the responsibility to create a NetLogo simulation to model our winch. Some of the challenges we faced was incorporating the FisherTech motor with the Vex pieces, since there was no built in method to do so, and the challenge of creating enough torque in the system while maintaining a reasonable speed of ascent. We overcame the motor challenge by makeshifting a frame to hold the motor in place by surrounding the motor with L-brackets, and we used mechanical advantage calculations to determine the desired gear ratio to use. This project taught me primarily how to build and assemble a machine, as I had no prior experience in doing so, and also taught me how to ask for help from other more experienced peers and improvise. This project helped me realize that it is important to learn new skills and try something I am not familiar with, along with teaching me how to delegate tasks to get the job done efficiently.
